META

a comprehensive list of every single change from the Heartstopper comics to the Netflix adaptation

Notes:

  • This is a living document and will be updated with Seasons 2 and 3.
  • All credits for images go to Alice Oseman and Netflix.
  • Minor edits to the dialogue will not be addressed.
  • No item on this list should be taken as criticism.
  • All trigger warnings applicable to the comics (Chapters 1-3) and the show may apply to this document.

General

  • all swearing removed, except for Harry Greene calling Charlie Spring a fag
  • all text messages happen over Instagram, not Facebook Messenger/otherwise
  • in comics Nick and Charlie start texting over Messenger, then move to iMessage; in show they only use Instagram
  • outfits modernized to fit current trends
  • dialogue modernized and shortened
  • expanded subplots for the other relationships (Tao Xu / Elle Argent, Tara Jones / Darcy Olsson) as well as friendships between characters (Elle/Tara/Darcy, Tao/Elle/Charlie/Isaac)
  • Aled Last removed, replaced with Isaac Henderson
  • Oliver Spring removed
  • Christian, Otis, and Sai not mentioned by name, all dialogue by them removed
  • Otis is mentioned in the subtitles (Episode 4)
  • Christian, Otis, and Sai have been individually cast
  • new character, Imogen, introduced
  • new character, Yan (Tao’s mum), introduced
  • majority of Charlie’s inner thoughts/monologues redacted, e.g.:
  • removed several instances of Charlie doubting or hating himself; results in Netflix-Charlie being happy where comic-Charlie would have been depressed
  • Charlie’s time in the rugby team is significantly dramatized
  • Nick is a lot less flirty
  • Mr. Ajayi is a teacher at Truham, not Higgs
  • effectively replaces the Sixth Formers who helped stopped the bullying against Charlie in Year 9
  • Tao has several confrontations with Harry et al.; in the comics they did not interact
  • timeframes remain obscure, whereas explicitly stated in comics e.g.:
