Thursday, April 6, 2023


National headlines

1. Janet Protasiewicz wins Wisconsin supreme court election

Mike De Sisti | Milwaukee Journal Sentinel

Progressives secured a long-awaited victory as the liberal judge won her election by an astounding 11% margin. Protasiewicz’s win comes at a crucial time for reproductive rights; conservative justices up to this point had held a majority in the state supreme court, and upcoming rulings on Wisconsin’s abortion law meant the future of abortion access had hinged on this election.

Protasiewicz previously served at the Milwaukee County Circuit Court, to which she was elected as a judge in 2014 — and before that, she was a prosecutor in Milwaukee County with a focus on domestic abuse and other violent crimes.

Source: Milwaukee Journal Sentinel

2. Taiwan’s president meets with Kevin McCarthy in Southern California

Frederic J. Brown | AFP

Tsai Ing-wen made a brief visit in Simi Valley, meeting with the U.S. Speaker of the House in what the New York Times reported was a carefully choreographed series of meetings. The move was an apparent attempt to avoid furthering tensions in the Taiwan Strait — already made sour when Nancy Pelosi, McCarthy’s predecessor, ignored threats of retaliation from China to visit the island.

Though the visit was more tame than the promises of unabashed defiance McCarthy had made during the midterm elections, he told reporters after the meetings that a visit of his own to Taiwan was not off the table.

I am the speaker of the House, he said. There is no place that China’s going to tell me where I can go and who I can speak to, whether you be foe or whether you be friend.

Tsai, who will continue a 10-day stint traveling to New York, Guatemala, and Belize, said the meeting would reassure the people of Taiwan that we are not isolated and we are not alone.

Source: Los Angeles Times, New York Times

3. Catholic priests in Maryland sexually abused over 600 children, state report shows

Kim Hairston | The Baltimore Sun

The Maryland attorney general's office on Wednesday released its report, concluding a lengthy investigation into 80 years of abuse and coverup.

The nearly 500-page report was the subject of intense legal battles over its release — until February, when a judge ordered a redacted version of the report be released, writing: [The] only form of justice that may now be available is a public reckoning.

These revelations come as ever frequent reports in recent years have brought systemic abuses in the Catholic Church to light. A 2019 CNN investigative report detailed harrowing accounts of abuse, by pedophile priests at a California school. Just across northern California, 1,500 lawsuits against the Catholic Church had been filed throughout the state's three-year lookback window, plaintiffs’ attorneys said.

Source: Associated Press


Latest in sports

1. NBA: Bucks take top seed with win over Bulls

Getty Images

Milwaukee took home an easy 105-92 victory against Chicago Wednesday night, and with it the best record in the league — a feat made more impressive by star forward Giannis Antetokounmpo’s absence, and Khris Middleton and Grayson Allen also being sidelined due to injuries. Bobby Portis Jr., Brook Lopez, and Jrue Holiday each stepped in with 27, 26, and 26 points, respectively.

The Bucks’ 58-22 record began as a 9-0, which eventually faltered in a mid-season slump, bringing them as low as fourth. From January to March, however, a 16-win streak — tied for third longest in franchise history — set the stage for complete dominance of the league.

2. NBA: Dallas scores long-awaited win amid streak of disappointments

Getty Images

The doomer headlines of the Mavericks having utterly blown their chances will at last be staved off for a little while, with a 123-119 win against the Kings reviving some enthusiasm for the once-upon-a-time playoff-hopefuls.

After a dismal first half, as usual — At one point, [Sacramento] missed 12 of 13 shots. But they found their rhythm, as teams often do against the Mavericks, read a Mavs Moneyball game recap — Kyrie Irving and Luka Doncic seemed to have finally found their own rhythm as well. Irving topped the scoreboard with 31 points, 19 of which were fourth quarter; Doncic followed suit with 29 and 10 rebounds.

While some are lamenting what appears to be a complete downfall after the Irving trade — their playoff chances dipped to 6% from 58% since the trade deadline — others wonder whether there’s still a chance to make the play-in.
