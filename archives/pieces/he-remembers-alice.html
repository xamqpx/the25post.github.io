It hurts to open his eyes. Bright white light pouring in from every direction; it was never this bright in the nursing home.

He lies there for a few minutes, lets his eyes adjust. Slowly the light fades, even still intolerably bright. It's the sun. This is definitely not the nursing home.

Rolling hills stretch for miles, flushed with overgrowth and daisies and sunflowers. Hummingbirds, butterflies, aged oak trees; the quiet rush of a river winding through the grass. The sky is a little too blue. Like this is a dream.

Where am I?

He has the energy to sit up, finally, then stand. He walks to the river, checks his reflection. The wrinkles are gone. His eyes aren't worn and drooping anymore. His hair is blonde, not gray. He checks his hands: perfectly smooth. His back: straight. And he remembers who he is again: his name was Will Akner. He had a wife, Alice. They had two children together. He remembers now.

Somehow, he's young again. This is definitely a dream. Either that, or—

"You're awake," a voice says from behind. Will jumps, turns around. It's a man, darker, with a buzz cut and wearing all black; slightly taller, slightly larger, beard perfectly trimmed. Too perfectly trimmed.

"Hi, Will," the man says. "I'm Lucas. I've been waiting for you."

Will freezes. His mind races through every possible thing he could say, and settles with: "Hi, um, what."

Afterlife. A second chance. "A second chance?" What bullshit. "Where's my wife? My kids?"
