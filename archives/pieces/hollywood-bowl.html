NON / FICTION

hollywood bowl, upper level

1 October 2024

milky paint-green tiles;
an assembly of urinals,
almost all occupied;
sour thick air hits
my face like a wall of lint;
the alcohol in all of our
bodies combined could
kill three cows. in me, two
solo cups of sauvignon blanc;
i’d floated toward an
open slot, feeling as if
each cell in my brain
was wrapped in a blanket.

the concert had just
ended, kind of -- the kind
where hundreds howled and
clapped and begged for encore
after encore. there was still
another encore. “ok, now this
is the last song,” she was saying,
her voice a drowning
echo from this humid stall.
and she began to sing.

two men also begin to hum
along, their voices vibrating
against each other like
ripples in water -- the wine
renders the reverb tenfold --
the simplest kind of harmony;
though, curiously, they still
refuse to look each other,
as is the unspoken code of
the men’s bathroom;

do they then hum for themselves?
to feel that satisfaction of a note
rising from the throat, ringing and
rattling the brain just so?
do they sing for each other, a
wordless acknowledgment of
brotherhood in an awkward
circumstance?

men are weird that way;
fleeting connections made
satisfy the self and the other,
before they go their separate

ways, having washed their
hands -- never to see
each other again.

just some minutes ago, i had been
howling, too, after the encore’s
encore; and the man in front
of me looked back and flashed
a knowing smile. we met eyes
only for a second; and we laughed

together -- chuckles lost in the
hundred other hoots and hollers.
we laughed at the absurdity of
my noises; we laughed with the
joy of living this alone life
lonely together, separate, shared.
we are the same, we laughed.
then he turned back again.
i don’t remember his face.
the wine was signaling to leave;
i needed to go to the bathroom. ◆
